# NAPPS Report — Benchmark Reference
*Sources: Littledata (2,800 Shopify stores, 2023), Opensend, MobiLoud, Metrilo, IRP Commerce, Blend Commerce. Last updated: 2025. All figures are Shopify/ecommerce-specific unless noted.*

---

## Important note on mobile vs. web benchmarks

NAPPS brands operate on **native mobile apps**, which typically convert **higher than mobile web** but **lower than desktop web**. The benchmarks below are for Shopify stores overall (blended mobile + desktop). When a brand's conversion rate looks low against these benchmarks, keep in mind that mobile web averages 1.2% on Shopify — a native app should be doing better than that, so the blended Shopify average is a reasonable floor, not a ceiling.

## Metrics Without Reliable Sector Benchmarks

For the following metrics, **benchmark by peer rank** within the NAPPS platform rather than against external figures. External data is either too variable or not reliably sector-segmented:

- **CLV** — too dependent on AOV, purchase frequency, and brand age to have a meaningful universal benchmark. Use peer rank + the 3:1 CLV:CAC ratio as a health signal if CAC data is available.
- **Churn Rate** — highly dependent on product category and repurchase cycle. Peer rank is more meaningful than a universal figure.
- **Sessions / Traffic** — absolute session counts vary too much by brand size and marketing spend. Peer rank only.
- **Total Sales / Total Orders** — absolute figures vary too much. Peer rank only.

**How to frame peer-rank-only metrics in narratives:**
> "Sessions rank 8th among 50 brands on the platform, placing this brand in the top 20% for traffic generation."
> "CLV ranks 31st out of 50, below the platform median — customers are spending less over their lifetime than the typical NAPPS brand."

## Metric Relationship Patterns
*When these combinations appear, call them out in the executive summary — cross-metric signals are more diagnostic than individual flags.*

| Pattern | Signal | Recommended framing |
|---|---|---|
| High sessions + low conversion | Traffic quality or UX problem | "Customers are arriving but not buying — the issue is likely in the product page or checkout experience, not acquisition." |
| Low sessions + high conversion | Acquisition bottleneck | "Strong product-market fit: the store converts well when traffic arrives. The priority is growing the audience." |
| High add-to-cart + high abandonment | Checkout friction or price sensitivity | "Intent is there — customers are adding to cart — but something is blocking completion. Checkout friction or unexpected costs are the likely culprits." |
| Low AOV + high repeat purchase | Loyal but low-value customers | "Strong loyalty signal, but customers are buying small. There's a significant upsell opportunity in this engaged base." |
| High AOV + low repeat purchase | High-value one-time buyers | "Each order is valuable, but customers aren't returning. Retention investment would have outsized impact here." |
| High refund rate + high conversion | Volume without quality | "Headline conversion looks strong, but returns are eroding real revenue. Net conversion (after returns) is the metric to watch." |
| Improving conversion + declining sessions | Efficiency up, funnel shrinking | "The store is getting better at converting what traffic it has, but the audience is shrinking. Both need attention." |
